Target: By 2030, reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70 per 100,000 live births.

# Load real TidyTuesday data
library(tidytuesdayR)

# Method 1: Using tidytuesdayR package (recommended)
tuesdata <- tt_load('2018-04-16')
mortality_data <- tuesdata$global_mortality

# Method 2: Direct URL (if package unavailable)
# mortality_data <- readr::read_csv('https://raw.githubusercontent.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/master/data/2018/2018-04-16/global_mortality.csv')

# Quick exploration
glimpse(mortality_data)

# Filter for maternal mortality and prepare data
maternal_mortality <- mortality_data %>%
  # Select maternal mortality column (percentage of deaths)
  select(country, country_code, year, 
         maternal_disorders = `Maternal disorders (%)`) %>%
  # Remove missing values
  filter(!is.na(maternal_disorders)) %>%
  # Add continent information
  mutate(
    continent = countrycode(country_code, "iso3c", "continent"),
    region = countrycode(country_code, "iso3c", "region"),
    # Classify income groups (simplified)
    income_group = case_when(
      continent == "Europe" ~ "High income",
      continent == "Americas" & country %in% c("United States", "Canada") ~ "High income",
      continent == "Africa" ~ "Low income",
      continent == "Asia" & country %in% c("Japan", "South Korea", "Singapore") ~ "High income",
      TRUE ~ "Middle income"
    )
  ) %>%
  # Remove rows with missing continent
  filter(!is.na(continent))

# Preview cleaned data
head(maternal_mortality, 10)

# Summary statistics
summary(maternal_mortality$maternal_disorders)

5. Annual Rate of Reduction

Code 
mmr_change <- maternal_mortality %>%
  filter(year %in% c(2000, 2023)) %>%
  select(country, continent, income_group, year, mmr) %>%
  pivot_wider(names_from = year, values_from = mmr, names_prefix = "mmr_") %>%
  mutate(
    annual_reduction_rate = ((mmr_2023 / mmr_2000)^(1/23) - 1) * 100,
    met_target = annual_reduction_rate <= -5.5  # Need ~5.5% annual reduction to meet SDG
  ) %>%
  filter(!is.na(continent))

ggplot(mmr_change, aes(x = annual_reduction_rate, fill = met_target)) +
  geom_histogram(bins = 30, alpha = 0.8, color = "white") +
  geom_vline(xintercept = -5.5, linetype = "dashed", color = "#4C9F38", size = 1.2) +
  annotate("text", x = -5.5, y = 20, label = "Target: -5.5% annually", 
           angle = 90, vjust = -0.5, color = "#4C9F38", fontface = "bold") +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("TRUE" = "#4C9F38", "FALSE" = "#E5243B"),
                    labels = c("TRUE" = "On track", "FALSE" = "Off track")) +
  scale_x_continuous(labels = percent_format(scale = 1)) +
  labs(
    title = "Distribution of Annual MMR Reduction Rates (2000-2023)",
    subtitle = "Most countries are not reducing MMR fast enough to meet SDG 3.1",
    x = "Annual Reduction Rate (%)",
    y = "Number of Countries",
    fill = "SDG Progress",
    caption = "Data: Simulated WHO patterns | #TidyTuesday"
  ) +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(face = "bold", size = 16),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 11, color = "gray40"),
    legend.position = "bottom"

ðŸ’¡ Key Findings

Critical Insights

  1. Off Track: At current rates, the world will not achieve SDG 3.1 by 2030. Global MMR needs to drop from ~220 to <70 in 7 years.

  2. Regional Inequality: Sub-Saharan Africa accounts for ~66% of global maternal deaths despite having only ~14% of global births.

  3. Slow Progress: Only ~15% of countries are reducing MMR at the required rate (>5.5% annually).

  4. Income Disparity: Low-income countries have MMRs 30-40x higher than high-income countries.

  5. Preventable Deaths: ~95% of maternal deaths are preventable with quality antenatal care, skilled birth attendance, and emergency obstetric care.

ðŸŽ¯ Evidence-Based Recommendations

Immediate Actions (2024-2025)

  1. Strengthen Health Systems
    • Train and deploy midwives to rural areas
    • Ensure 24/7 emergency obstetric care at district hospitals
    • Improve referral systems for complications
  2. Increase Funding
    • Double maternal health budgets in high-burden countries
    • Prioritize countries with MMR >300
  3. Address Social Determinants
    • Increase girlsâ€™ education (each year of schooling reduces MMR by 5-10%)
    • Reduce child marriage
    • Improve nutrition programs for pregnant women

Long-term Strategies (2025-2030)

  1. Universal Health Coverage
    • Remove financial barriers to maternal health services
    • Provide free antenatal care and delivery
  2. Data Systems
    • Strengthen civil registration and vital statistics
    • Implement maternal death surveillance and response systems
  3. Community Engagement
    • Train community health workers
    • Engage traditional birth attendants in referral systems

## ðŸ’¡ Key Findings

::: {.callout-warning}
## Critical Insights

1. **Off Track**: At current rates, the world will **not** achieve SDG 3.1 by 2030. Global MMR needs to drop from ~220 to <70 in 7 years.

2. **Regional Inequality**: Sub-Saharan Africa accounts for ~66% of global maternal deaths despite having only ~14% of global births.

3. **Slow Progress**: Only ~15% of countries are reducing MMR at the required rate (>5.5% annually).

4. **Income Disparity**: Low-income countries have MMRs **30-40x higher** than high-income countries.

5. **Preventable Deaths**: ~95% of maternal deaths are preventable with quality antenatal care, skilled birth attendance, and emergency obstetric care.
:::

## ðŸŽ¯ Evidence-Based Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (2024-2025)

1. **Strengthen Health Systems**
   - Train and deploy midwives to rural areas
   - Ensure 24/7 emergency obstetric care at district hospitals
   - Improve referral systems for complications

2. **Increase Funding**
   - Double maternal health budgets in high-burden countries
   - Prioritize countries with MMR >300

3. **Address Social Determinants**
   - Increase girls' education (each year of schooling reduces MMR by 5-10%)
   - Reduce child marriage
   - Improve nutrition programs for pregnant women

### Long-term Strategies (2025-2030)

4. **Universal Health Coverage**
   - Remove financial barriers to maternal health services
   - Provide free antenatal care and delivery

5. **Data Systems**
   - Strengthen civil registration and vital statistics
   - Implement maternal death surveillance and response systems

6. **Community Engagement**
   - Train community health workers
   - Engage traditional birth attendants in referral systems
