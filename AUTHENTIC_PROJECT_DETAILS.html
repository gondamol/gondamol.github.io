Key Insights from Dashboard

1. Lost to Follow-Up Crisis

  • >60% of cancer patients lost to follow-up
  • Major barrier to treatment completion
  • System failure in continuity of care
  • Implications for survival outcomes

2. Financial Barriers

  • High out-of-pocket expenditure
  • Catastrophic health costs forcing treatment abandonment
  • Financial constraints on patients AND families
  • Economic burden extends beyond patient
  • Insurance gap analysis needed

3. Mental Health Dimension

  • Significant mental health issues among cancer patients
  • Psychological distress in patients and caregivers
  • Depression, anxiety, hopelessness
  • Lack of psycho-oncology services
  • Mental health support gaps in cancer care pathway

4. Young Population Affected

  • Unexpected proportion of young people with cancer
  • Different from typical age distribution in high-income countries
  • Implications for years of life lost
  • Economic impact on productive population
  • Need for age-specific interventions

5. High Esophageal Cancer Burden

  • Very high esophageal cancer rates
  • Particular problem in certain regions (Nyanza)
  • Dietary and environmental risk factors
  • Late-stage diagnosis common
  • Poor prognosis
  • Geographic clustering patterns

6. HIV-Related Cancers

  • Significant burden of HIV-associated cancers
  • Kaposi‚Äôs sarcoma
  • Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
  • Cervical cancer (HPV/HIV interaction)
  • Double burden: HIV + cancer
  • Treatment complexity

7. Pediatric Cancer Burden

  • Children‚Äôs cancers significant
  • Leukemias, lymphomas, Wilms tumor
  • Family devastation
  • Treatment abandonment due to costs
  • Specialized care needs
  • Long-term survivor care challenges
